# Proposal: Radix DAO Phase 1 Governance (12-Month Transition)

> **Historical rationale document.** This is the original proposal that established the Phase 1 governance framework. The normative Phase 1 rules are defined in `Transition-Governance-Framework.md`. Where this proposal and that framework conflict, the framework prevails. The working group structure described in §4 reflects early-stage thinking; the approved Phase 1 structure comprises 5 Working Groups as defined in the framework.

---

## 1. Summary

This proposal establishes a **simplified governance framework for the first 12 months** of the Radix DAO.

The objective is to:

* Enable rapid and safe transition from the Foundation
* Reduce governance complexity during early operations
* Maintain decentralization while ensuring operational effectiveness

This Phase 1 model is **temporary** and will evolve into a more complete governance system after the initial period.

---

## 2. Why a Simplified Phase

Launching a DAO with full governance complexity from day one introduces:

* operational friction
* decision delays
* coordination challenges

This proposal adopts a **progressive decentralization approach**:

> Start simple → operate → learn → evolve

---

## 3. Core Principles (Unchanged)

The following principles remain fully enforced:

* Token holder sovereignty
* Non-distribution of assets
* Asset lock on dissolution
* Transparency and accountability
* Separation of governance, execution, and oversight

---

## 4. Governance Structure (Simplified)

---

### 🗳️ DAO (Token Holders)

* Final decision-making authority
* Approves all binding proposals

---

### 👥 Working Groups (Reduced)

Initial Working Groups:

* Product & Ecosystem
* Community & Growth
* Governance & Operations

Responsibilities:

* coordinate execution
* propose initiatives
* manage approved budgets

---

### 🔄 Strategic Coordination

* Lightweight coordination function
* May be embedded within Governance WG

Purpose:

* align priorities
* synthesize strategy inputs

---

### 🏛️ Accountability Council (RAC)

Acts as:

* governance process guardian
* transitional coordination layer

---

### 🔐 Authorized Signers

* execute treasury transactions
* sign agreements
* operate under multisig

---

### ⚖️ Legal Entity

* DAO LLC
* holds assets
* enables legal operation

---

## 5. Proposal System (Simplified)

Two proposal types:

### 5.1 Binding Proposals

* budget allocations
* governance changes
* major decisions

---

### 5.2 Signaling Proposals

* community direction
* non-binding decisions

---

## 6. Voting (Simplified)

* Token-weighted voting (XRD and LSU converted to XRD-equivalent at snapshot)
* Single quorum rule (defined in parameters)
* Simple majority approval

---

## 7. Execution Model (Unchanged Core)

* Working Groups initiate execution
* Authorized Signers execute
* RAC verifies compliance

---

## 8. Transitional Delegated Authority (RAC)

During the 12-month period, the RAC is granted **limited delegated authority** to support operations.

---

### 8.1 RAC May:

* resolve minor governance ambiguities
* facilitate execution where proposals lack detail
* coordinate urgent operational decisions
* support system setup and transition

---

### 8.2 RAC May NOT:

* override DAO decisions
* define strategy independently
* allocate treasury outside approved proposals
* modify governance structures without DAO approval

---

### 8.3 Accountability

* all actions publicly reported
* subject to DAO review
* DAO retains full override authority

---

### 8.4 Sunset Clause

* expires after 12 months
* renewal requires DAO vote

---

## 9. Treasury & Execution (Simplified)

* multisig custody required
* 3–5 authorized signers
* execution must follow approved proposals

---

## 10. Emergency Handling

* RAC may act under defined emergency conditions
* actions must be:

  * minimal
  * temporary
  * fully disclosed

---

## 11. Legal & Foundation Alignment

This framework satisfies Foundation requirements:

* aligned objectives
* non-distribution principle
* asset lock
* governance clarity
* identifiable signers
* secure custody

---

## 12. What is Deferred to Phase 2

The following will be expanded later:

* detailed proposal categories
* advanced parameter tuning
* expanded working group structure
* advanced dispute mechanisms
* more granular governance processes

---

## 13. Transition Timeline

Phase 1 duration: 12 months

During this period:

* governance operates in simplified mode
* community gains experience
* improvements are identified

---

## 14. Phase 2 Transition

Before end of Phase 1:

* review governance performance
* propose full governance framework
* DAO votes on next iteration

---

## 15. Risks & Mitigation

---

### Risk: Over-centralization of RAC

Mitigation:

* strict limits
* sunset clause
* DAO override

---

### Risk: Operational inefficiency

Mitigation:

* simplified processes
* delegated coordination

---

### Risk: Governance immaturity

Mitigation:

* phased approach

---

## 16. Proposal

The community is asked to:

1. Approve the Phase 1 governance framework
2. Authorize establishment of DAO legal entity
3. Initiate transition from Foundation
4. Begin operations under simplified model

---

## 17. Guiding Statement

> This Phase 1 DAO is designed to be:
>
> * simple enough to operate
> * strong enough to protect assets
> * flexible enough to evolve

---

## 18. Call for Feedback

Community input is encouraged to:

* refine scope of RAC delegation
* adjust parameters
* ensure alignment with ecosystem needs

---

**This is the first step toward a fully decentralized Radix DAO.**
