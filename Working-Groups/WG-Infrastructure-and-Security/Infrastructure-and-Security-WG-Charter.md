# Infrastructure & Security Working Group Charter

---

## 1. Purpose

This Working Group exists to operate and protect the DAO's technical infrastructure, enforce information security and compliance standards, manage network and node operations, and coordinate emergency response. It acts as the DAO's CIO/CISO function.

It does not own software development, repository governance, or smart contract audit coordination — those responsibilities sit with the Protocol & Engineering WG.

---

## 2. Scope

### In Scope

* Security monitoring of DAO-controlled infrastructure (governance platform, treasury multisig, gateway operations, Stokenet/testnet)
* Infrastructure operations — service level monitoring and compliance oversight for all DAO-funded node and gateway operators
* Coordination of the Security Disclosure & Bug Bounty Policy (once formally activated)
* Information security compliance — defining and enforcing security standards across DAO-operated systems
* Emergency coordination — acting as the technical interface for the RAC during security incidents
* Security incident documentation and post-mortems
* Review and approval of infrastructure proposals (PROP-001, PROP-002, and successors) for security and compliance

### Out of Scope

* Cannot unilaterally suspend DAO operations — emergency suspension requires RAC approval per Emergency & Safeguards Policy
* Cannot make independent treasury disbursements for security responses without governance authorization (except as permitted under Emergency & Safeguards Policy)
* Cannot override governance decisions on infrastructure contracts
* Cannot act as the sole arbiter of vulnerability severity — classification follows the Security Disclosure & Bug Bounty Policy
* Does not coordinate smart contract audits — that responsibility sits with the Protocol & Engineering WG
* Does not govern source repositories or define secure development lifecycle practices — owned by the Protocol & Engineering WG

---

## 3. Responsibilities

* Maintain a security monitoring framework for DAO-controlled systems and infrastructure
* Oversee SLA compliance for all DAO-funded node and gateway operators; escalate failures
* Triage and coordinate response to reported vulnerabilities per the Security Disclosure & Bug Bounty Policy
* Define information security standards and compliance requirements that Protocol & Engineering WG must meet within its development lifecycle
* Act as technical point of contact for emergency coordination with the RAC
* Manage the DAO's bug bounty programme (upon formal activation of the Security Disclosure & Bug Bounty Policy)
* Publish quarterly security and infrastructure status reports
* Review infrastructure proposals for security compliance before DAO vote

---

## 4. Deliverables

* Quarterly security and infrastructure status reports (system health, incidents, disclosures handled)
* Infrastructure SLA monitoring reports (monthly, covering all DAO-funded operator SLAs)
* Security incident post-mortems (published within 30 days of incident resolution)
* Information security compliance standards (published at WG activation; updated as required)
* Bug bounty programme administration (upon policy activation)

---

## 5. Leadership Structure

* 2–3 elected Stewards
* Term: 6 months
* Elected via DAO governance

---

## 6. Steward Responsibilities

* Coordinate security monitoring, infrastructure oversight, and compliance activities
* Act as primary point of contact for vulnerability reporters and the bug bounty programme
* Liaise with the RAC during security incidents and emergency operations
* Define and maintain information security standards
* Publish required reports on schedule
* Maintain operational security standards for internal WG communications

---

## 7. Decision-Making

* Internal: simple majority of seated Stewards
* Quorum: 2 of 3 (or majority if 2 seated)

DAO approval required for:

* Any new infrastructure service contract or modification
* Formal activation of the bug bounty programme
* Suspension of any DAO-funded infrastructure service

Emergency coordination decisions (time-critical, within RAC emergency framework):

* Stewards may act as technical advisory body to the RAC under Emergency & Safeguards Policy
* Stewards do not have independent authority to suspend or modify infrastructure without RAC authorization

---

## 8. Budget Authority

* Fund security tooling, monitoring services, and infrastructure oversight within DAO-approved budget
* Coordinate bug bounty payouts (upon policy activation and governance authorization)
* No independent spending authority for emergency responses beyond DAO-approved limits

---

## 9. Reporting

* Quarterly security and infrastructure status reports — aligned with Working Group reporting schedule
* Infrastructure SLA reports — monthly, covering all DAO-funded operator SLAs
* Incident post-mortems — published within 30 days of resolution
* Emergency disclosures — published to governance forum within 48 hours of emergency action (per Emergency & Safeguards Policy)

---

## 10. Coordination

* RAC (emergency coordination; sole authority to invoke emergency operations suspension)
* Protocol & Engineering WG (security standards input for development lifecycle; incident notification on deployments)
* Treasury & Finance WG (bug bounty payouts, infrastructure budget administration)
* Governance & Legal WG (policy activation sequencing — Security Disclosure, Bug Bounty Policy)
* All infrastructure operators (PROP-001, PROP-002, and successors)

---

## 11. Conflicts of Interest

Stewards must:

* Disclose any personal, financial, or organisational relationship with infrastructure operators or security vendors they oversee
* Recuse themselves from decisions where a conflict exists
* Avoid self-dealing in vendor selection or bug bounty adjudication

Stewards who are also infrastructure operators must not participate in security reviews of their own infrastructure. All disclosures must be public and logged.

---

## 12. Renewal / Sunset

* Standard term: 6 months
* Renewal requires a new DAO governance proposal with updated charter, budget, and performance summary
* If not renewed, open security incidents and monitoring responsibilities transfer to the RAC within 21 days; active infrastructure contracts are documented and handed to the successor WG or RAC
* DAO may dissolve this WG at any time by governance vote; all spending authority ceases immediately; RAC assumes emergency coordination responsibilities until a successor is established

---

## 13. Guiding Principle

> This Working Group exists to **keep the DAO's infrastructure running, secure, and compliant** —
> operating as a professional CIO/CISO function that monitors, enforces, and responds,
> without reaching into development or protocol decisions.
