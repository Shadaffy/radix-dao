# Working Group Framework

## 1. Purpose

Working Groups are established by the DAO to:

* Execute DAO-approved priorities
* Coordinate contributors and initiatives
* Manage workstreams within defined mandates
* Report progress and outcomes to the DAO

Working Groups are **execution bodies**, not governance authorities.

---

## 2. Authority Boundaries

Working Groups:

* DO execute within approved mandates
* DO manage approved budgets
* DO coordinate contributors and issue RFPs

Working Groups:

* DO NOT define or own DAO strategy
* DO NOT override DAO governance decisions
* DO NOT bypass governance processes
* DO NOT control treasury outside approved limits

All authority remains with token-holder governance.

---

## 3. Creation of Working Groups

A Working Group must be approved by DAO governance.

A proposal must include:

* Purpose and rationale
* Scope and responsibilities
* Draft charter
* Initial budget request
* Success metrics
* Proposed term

---

## 4. Dissolution

A Working Group may be dissolved by DAO vote.

Upon dissolution:

* All spending authority ceases
* Unspent funds return to treasury
* All records and work must be archived publicly

---

## 5. Leadership Structure

Each Working Group has **2–3 elected Stewards**.

Stewards:

* Are accountable for the group’s outcomes
* Coordinate execution (not necessarily execute themselves)
* Serve fixed terms

---

## 6. Elections & Terms

* Stewards are elected via DAO governance
* Terms are fixed (e.g., 6 months)
* Re-election is allowed
* Vacancies trigger replacement elections

Stewards may be removed at any time by DAO vote.

---

## 7. Budget & Spending

Working Groups:

* Operate within DAO-approved budgets
* May issue RFPs and fund contributors within mandate

Working Groups may NOT:

* Exceed approved budgets
* Commit treasury beyond authorized limits
* Create new financial obligations without approval

### 7.1 Budget Variance

Minor operational variance is permitted within these limits:

* Up to **5%** of the approved budget may be exceeded without prior approval, provided it is disclosed in the next biweekly report
* Variance between **5% and 15%** requires RAC notification within 5 business days and must be ratified by a Treasury proposal within the current budget period
* Any variance **exceeding 15%** constitutes a breach of mandate and requires immediate suspension of non-essential spending until a new Treasury proposal is approved

Unspent funds at the end of a budget period must be returned to treasury unless a carry-forward is explicitly approved by governance.

---

## 8. Reporting & Transparency

Each Working Group must:

* Publish regular updates (recommended: biweekly)
* Maintain a public dashboard
* Provide quarterly retrospectives

Status should be tracked as:

* On Track
* At Risk
* Off Track
* Completed

---

## 9. Conflicts of Interest

Stewards must:

* Disclose conflicts of interest
* Recuse themselves where applicable
* Avoid self-dealing

All disclosures must be public.

---

## 10. Relationship to RAC

* RAC ensures Working Groups follow governance rules
* RAC does not manage day-to-day execution
* Disputes escalate through governance processes

---

## 11. Sub-Groups & Workstreams

Working Groups may:

* Create sub-groups
* Initiate temporary workstreams

These must:

* Remain within mandate
* Be transparently documented

---

## 12. Amendment

This framework may be updated via DAO governance.

Individual Working Group charters must align with this framework.
