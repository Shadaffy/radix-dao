## Section 3 — Non-Distribution Commitment

The Company operates on a strict non-distribution basis. In accordance with the Company's Charter §10 and Legal Wrapper & Representation §5:

* All income, assets, and property of the Company are applied solely toward the advancement of the Company's stated objectives
* No part of the Company's income or assets is distributed to Members, Delegates, or token holders in their capacity as such
* Reasonable compensation for services rendered and reimbursement of legitimate operational expenses are permitted

This non-distribution commitment is permanent and survives any change in membership, governance structure, or operational phase. It may not be altered without a Constitutional Governance Proposal meeting the approval threshold specified in the DAO Parameters Registry.

---

## Section 4 — Asset Stewardship

Assets received by the Company from any Asset Transferor are held in trust for the benefit of the decentralized ecosystem the Company governs. They are not held for the benefit of the Company's Members or Delegates.

The Company commits to:

* Managing received assets in accordance with its Charter, Operating Agreement, and adopted governance framework
* Applying the intellectual property and open-source obligations of its IP Policy (§10) to all transferred assets, including maintaining applicable licenses and preserving attribution
* Maintaining accurate provenance records for all received assets as required by Operating Agreement §9.3
* Publishing a public accounting of all received assets and their proposed disposition prior to any dissolution asset transfer, in accordance with Operating Agreement §9.3

In the event of dissolution, remaining assets — including any assets received from an Asset Transferor — shall be transferred to a successor entity meeting the qualifying criteria defined in Operating Agreement §9.2, and shall not be distributed to Members or token holders. Prior to any such transfer, the Company shall provide written notice to any primary Asset Transferor whose transferred assets constitute a material portion of the Company's holdings.

## Section 5 — Governance Authority

On-chain governance decisions made through the Company's governance smart contract (identified in Operating Agreement §1.4) constitute legally binding decisions of the LLC membership under the Marshall Islands DAO Act §708 and Operating Agreement §4.3.

Asset management, treasury actions, and IP enforcement decisions require Governance Proposal approval in accordance with the Proposal & Voting Framework and Execution & Treasury Actions Policy, as adopted under Operating Agreement §12.4.

No single Member, Delegate, or body holds unilateral authority over the Company's assets. Treasury actions require multi-signature authorization by no fewer than three qualified Delegates under the Authorized Signers Rules.
