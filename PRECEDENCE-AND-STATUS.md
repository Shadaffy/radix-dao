# Precedence and Document Status

This document is the authoritative statement of the governance document hierarchy and the operative status of each normative document in this repository. It supersedes any conflicting hierarchy description in README, Charter, Supporting Materials, or the Role and Authority Map.

---

## 1. Conflict-Resolution Hierarchy

In the event of any conflict between documents in this repository, the following order of precedence applies:

1. **Applicable Marshall Islands law** — the Marshall Islands Limited Liability Company Act (52 MIRC Ch. 3) and the Marshall Islands DAO Act (52 MIRC Ch. 7) take precedence over all governance documents on matters of statutory compliance, entity formation, membership rights, and dissolution.

2. **The operative Operating Agreement** — once formally adopted by Governance Proposal, controls over all governance-layer documents on matters of entity formation, membership, statutory compliance, Delegated Functions, and any matter not expressly delegated to the governance framework. Until adoption, the Transition Governance Framework governs.

3. **The Charter** — controls over all governance policy documents on all constitutional governance matters: the DAO's purpose, core principles, authority structure, the non-distribution principle, and permanent guarantees.

4. **Governance policy documents** — control within their expressly delegated scope. Where two governance policy documents conflict, the more specific or more recently adopted document prevails; if still unresolved, the RAC submits a clarifying proposal within 14 days.

5. **The DAO Parameters Registry** — controls on all parameter values (quorum thresholds, approval percentages, time durations, budget limits, RAC composition). Descriptive statements of parameter values in any other document are summaries only and yield to the Registry.

6. **Explanatory and reference documents** — the Glossary, FAQ, Role and Authority Map, governance-body-intro, and Supporting Materials are non-normative. They are provided for navigation and onboarding and do not create enforceable obligations. Where they conflict with a normative document, the normative document prevails.

## 2. Normative vs. Explanatory Documents

| Category | Documents | Normative? |
|---|---|---|
| Formation | Certificate of Formation, Operating Agreement (once adopted) | Yes |
| Constitutional | Charter | Yes |
| Governance Policies | All documents in `Governance-Processes/` | Yes |
| Legal Policy | Legal Wrapper & Representation | Yes |
| Parameters | DAO Parameters Registry | Yes (parameters only) |
| Transition | Transition Governance Framework, Activation Roadmap | Yes (during transition period) |
| Reference / Explanatory | Glossary, FAQ, Role and Authority Map, governance-body-intro, Supporting Materials | **No** — explanatory only |

## 4. Maintenance

This document must be updated whenever:

- A document's status changes (e.g., Operating Agreement is formally adopted)
- A new normative document is created or deprecated
- A governance phase transition occurs

The RAC is responsible for proposing updates to this document via a Governance Process proposal.
