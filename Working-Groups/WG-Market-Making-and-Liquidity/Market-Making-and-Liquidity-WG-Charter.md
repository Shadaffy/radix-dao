# Market Making & Liquidity Working Group Charter

> **Status: Optional Working Group**
>
> This Working Group is not part of the five Phase 1 Working Groups established at DAO launch.
> It requires a separate DAO governance proposal to activate. Until that proposal passes,
> this charter is a published draft — available for community review and discussion but
> carrying no operational authority and no budget.
>
> Activation also requires completion of the legal review defined in §13 before any
> funds may be deployed.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Working Group exists to improve and sustain the liquidity and market quality of XRD
and other DAO-designated assets. It does so by:

* Sourcing, onboarding, and overseeing professional market-making service providers
* Monitoring liquidity health across approved venues
* Publishing transparent performance data to the DAO

It does not trade speculatively or manage treasury assets beyond the liquidity mandate.

---

## 2. Scope

### In Scope

* Issuing and evaluating RFPs for professional market-making services
* Negotiating and managing agreements with approved market-making counterparties
* Defining and monitoring liquidity performance metrics (spread, depth, uptime)
* Reporting liquidity health to the DAO on a biweekly basis
* Coordinating with the Ecosystem Growth WG on exchange listing initiatives
* Recommending changes to the approved venue list for DAO approval
* Managing the approved liquidity budget within the limits set by governance

### Out of Scope

* Cannot trade on its own account or make directional market bets
* Cannot approve new trading venues without a DAO governance vote
* Cannot commit to counterparty agreements exceeding the approved budget without a Treasury proposal
* Cannot reallocate funds outside the liquidity mandate without governance approval
* Cannot define DAO strategy independently

---

## 3. Responsibilities

* Conduct market and venue research to identify liquidity gaps and opportunities
* Issue transparent RFPs for market-making service providers
* Evaluate counterparty proposals against defined criteria (performance, risk, cost)
* Recommend counterparties to the DAO for approval on all agreements above a material threshold (defined in DAO Parameters)
* Execute agreements with approved counterparties via Authorized Signers
* Define and maintain a liquidity risk framework (acceptable spread ranges, minimum depth targets, venue concentration limits)
* Monitor counterparty performance against agreed SLAs and escalate failures
* Maintain a public dashboard of liquidity metrics
* Conduct regular counterparty reviews and recommend renewals or terminations

---

## 4. Deliverables

* Liquidity strategy document (published at WG activation)
* RFP framework and counterparty evaluation criteria (published within 30 days of activation)
* Counterparty agreement register (public, updated on each change)
* Biweekly liquidity health report (spread, depth, uptime by venue)
* Liquidity risk framework (published within 60 days of activation)
* Quarterly counterparty performance review
* Quarterly retrospective against mandate objectives

---

## 5. Leadership Structure

* 2–3 elected Stewards
* Term: 6 months
* Elected via DAO governance at the time of WG activation
* Stewards may be re-elected; removal requires a DAO governance vote

---

## 6. Steward Responsibilities

Stewards are responsible for:

* Coordinating all WG activities and contributor workstreams
* Owning the RFP and counterparty selection process
* Monitoring liquidity performance and escalating SLA failures
* Publishing all required reports on schedule
* Maintaining and updating the liquidity risk framework
* Escalating regulatory or legal concerns to the Governance & Legal WG immediately
* Reporting to the DAO and to the RAC

Stewards are accountable for outcomes but are not required to execute all work personally.
Contributors and specialist advisors may be engaged within the approved budget.

---

## 7. Decision-Making

* Internal decisions: simple majority of Stewards
* Quorum: 2 of 3 (or both Stewards if only 2 seated)

DAO approval required for:

* Onboarding any new market-making counterparty above the material threshold
* Approving or removing trading venues from the approved list
* Budget increases or scope expansion
* Changes to this charter
* Activation or dissolution of the WG

RAC notification required for:

* Any budget variance between 5% and 15% (must be ratified by Treasury proposal within the budget period)
* Any counterparty agreement that cannot be publicly disclosed (must be disclosed retrospectively within 30 days)

---

## 8. Budget Authority

* Budget source: DAO-approved allocation
* Allowed spend: market-making service fees, liquidity provision costs, tooling, and contributor compensation — all within the approved budget
* Unspent funds at term end must be returned to treasury unless carry-forward is explicitly approved by governance

Budget variance rules apply (Working Group Framework §7.1):

| Variance | Action |
|---|---|
| Up to 5% | Disclose in next biweekly report |
| 5%–15% | Notify RAC within 5 business days; ratify via Treasury proposal |
| Above 15% | Immediate spending freeze until new Treasury proposal is approved |

---

## 9. Reporting

* **Biweekly**: liquidity health update — spread, depth, uptime by venue; any counterparty SLA failures; budget status
* **Monthly**: treasury snapshot contribution (coordinate with Strategic Coordination WG)
* **Quarterly**: full counterparty performance review; budget actuals vs plan; risk framework status; goals vs outcomes
* **End of term**: full retrospective including budget reconciliation and handover notes

All reports must be published publicly on the DAO governance forum.

---

## 10. Conflicts of Interest

Given the financial nature of this WG's mandate, conflict of interest standards are elevated:

* Stewards must disclose any personal or financial relationship with any market-making counterparty, trading venue, or related entity — at onboarding and on an ongoing basis
* A Steward with a conflict in any counterparty selection or contract renewal must recuse from that decision; the remaining Stewards must document the recusal
* Stewards may not hold positions, advisory roles, or equity in any active or prospective counterparty without prior RAC review and community disclosure
* Stewards may not receive compensation from any counterparty beyond what is disclosed and approved by the DAO
* All disclosures must be public and logged in the WG's conflict of interest register

The RAC may audit the conflict of interest register at any time.

---

## 11. Coordination

This Working Group must coordinate with:

* **Strategic Coordination WG** — treasury oversight, cross-WG programme coordination, and monthly budget reconciliation
* **Governance & Legal WG** — legal review of all market-making activities prior to first deployment; ongoing regulatory monitoring; counterparty agreement review
* **Ecosystem Growth WG** — alignment on exchange listing priorities and ecosystem liquidity needs
* **Product & Protocol WG** — understanding of protocol events (upgrades, launches) that affect liquidity planning
* **RAC** — compliance monitoring, variance escalation, and charter adherence

---

## 12. Venue & Counterparty Governance

### 12.1 Approved Venue List

The DAO maintains an approved list of trading venues where this WG may operate. The initial list must be approved as part of the WG activation proposal. Additions or removals require a DAO governance vote.

### 12.2 Counterparty Onboarding

All market-making counterparties must:

* Pass an RFP process with published evaluation criteria
* Be disclosed to the DAO prior to engagement
* Be approved by the DAO for any agreement above the material threshold (defined in DAO Parameters)
* Sign agreements executed by Authorized Signers via multisig

### 12.3 Counterparty Offboarding

If a counterparty fails to meet SLA commitments:

1. The WG issues a formal performance notice (disclosed publicly within 48 hours)
2. If unresolved within the notice period defined in the agreement, the WG recommends termination
3. Termination is executed by Authorized Signers; DAO is notified within 72 hours

---

## 13. Activation Gate — Legal Review

**No liquidity funds may be deployed until the following condition is met:**

The Governance & Legal WG must publish a legal review confirming that the proposed market-making activities comply with applicable laws and regulations in the jurisdictions relevant to the DAO's legal wrapper and the planned venues.

This review must:

* Be published publicly
* Be completed before the first Authorized Signer execution request is submitted
* Be updated if the approved venue list changes materially

If the legal review identifies conditions or restrictions, those restrictions are automatically incorporated into this charter and the WG mandate until amended by governance.

---

## 14. Risks & Safeguards

### Risk: Counterparty Failure

Mitigation: SLA monitoring; public performance reporting; clear termination process; no single-counterparty concentration above a defined limit (to be set in the liquidity risk framework)

### Risk: Regulatory Exposure

Mitigation: Mandatory legal review before first deployment (§13); ongoing regulatory monitoring by Governance & Legal WG; immediate escalation obligation on Stewards

### Risk: Conflicts of Interest in Counterparty Selection

Mitigation: Elevated COI standards (§10); RAC audit rights; all counterparty decisions above material threshold require DAO approval

### Risk: Budget Overrun

Mitigation: Budget variance rules (§8); biweekly budget reporting; spending freeze trigger at >15% variance

### Risk: Centralization of Liquidity Decisions

Mitigation: Venue list governed by DAO vote; major counterparty decisions above material threshold require DAO approval; all agreements publicly disclosed

---

## 15. Renewal / Sunset

* Standard term: 6 months
* Renewal requires a new DAO governance proposal with updated charter, budget request, and performance summary
* If not renewed, all active counterparty agreements must be wound down and unspent funds returned to treasury within 30 days
* The DAO may dissolve this WG at any time by governance vote; upon dissolution, all spending authority ceases immediately

---

## 16. Guiding Principle

> This Working Group exists to **deepen and sustain market liquidity for the Radix ecosystem**,
> operating transparently and within strict governance boundaries —
> not to speculate, centralise financial decisions, or act beyond its mandate.
