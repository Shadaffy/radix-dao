# Legal Wrapper & Representation

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines:

* The legal structure through which the DAO operates
* How the DAO interacts with external parties
* Who is authorized to act on behalf of the DAO
* How assets are held and controlled

---

## 2. Legal Entity

The DAO operates through a legally recognized entity:

* Structure: Marshall Islands DAO LLC, incorporated under the Marshall Islands Limited Liability Company Act and the Marshall Islands DAO Act
* Governing Law: The laws of the Republic of the Marshall Islands govern this entity and the interpretation of this document
* Purpose: To act as the legal interface of the DAO

The legal entity:

* Holds assets on behalf of the DAO
* Enters into legal agreements
* Enables regulatory compliance where required

---

## 2A. Token Holder Membership

Governance token holders are members of the DAO LLC by virtue of their token holdings, in accordance with the Marshall Islands DAO Act. Membership rights are exercised through the governance framework established in the Charter and related governance documents. On-chain governance decisions constitute legally binding decisions of the LLC membership.

---

## 3. Relationship to the DAO

The legal entity:

* Acts **on behalf of the DAO**
* Must follow DAO-approved governance decisions
* Has no independent authority beyond defined mandates

---

## 4. Objectives Alignment

The legal entity must operate in alignment with the DAO’s purpose:

* Development of decentralized technologies
* Advancement of cryptographic systems
* Education and research in blockchain technologies
* Governance of decentralized networks

---

## 5. Non-Distribution Principle

All income and assets must:

* Be used solely to advance the DAO’s objectives
* Not be distributed to token holders or members

Exceptions allowed for:

* Reasonable compensation for services
* Reimbursement of expenses
* Legitimate operational costs

---

## 6. Asset Lock & Wind-Down

In the event of dissolution:

* Remaining assets must be transferred to an entity with substantially similar objectives, in accordance with the dissolution requirements of the Marshall Islands Limited Liability Company Act
* No assets may be distributed to members or token holders upon dissolution

---

## 7. Authorized Signers

### 7.1 Role

Authorized Signers are responsible for:

* Executing treasury transactions
* Signing legal agreements
* Managing custody systems

---

### 7.2 Requirements

Authorized Signers must:

* Be approved via DAO governance
* Meet KYC requirements where necessary
* Operate under multi-signature control

---

### 7.3 Constraints

Authorized Signers:

* Act only within DAO-approved mandates
* Cannot make independent strategic decisions
* Are accountable to the DAO

---

### 7.4 Operational Standards

Authorized Signers must comply with approved operational rules, including:

* response times
* recordkeeping
* security standards
* conflict disclosures
* continuity obligations

---

## 8. Manager Structure (Transition Phase)

During the Phase 1 transition period, 3–5 Managers are designated in accordance with the Marshall Islands DAO Act. Managers act as the legal representatives of the LLC for the purpose of executing DAO-approved decisions and maintaining entity standing. Managers are not beneficial owners of the DAO's assets and hold no authority independent of DAO governance decisions. Managers benefit from the limited liability protections afforded to LLC managers under Marshall Islands law.

Responsibilities include:

* Signing continuity agreements
* Maintaining legal entity standing
* Supporting asset transfer

---

## 9. Custody & Asset Control

The DAO must maintain secure custody systems:

* Multi-signature control required
* Institutional-grade custody preferred

Custody arrangements must:

* Be auditable
* Protect against unauthorized access
* Support operational needs

---

## 10. Confidential Information

Authorized Signer identities are **publicly disclosed** as part of the election and appointment process, in accordance with the Elections & Role Governance Policy.

The following operational details may remain confidential:

* Key management and custody procedures
* Security protocols and infrastructure details
* Specific custody provider arrangements

Such confidentiality is permitted solely for:

* Operational security (OpSec)
* Legal compliance

Identities themselves may not be kept private on the basis of these exceptions.

---

## 11. Web2 Assets & Credentials

The DAO must:

* Securely manage Web2 credentials
* Use controlled access systems
* Maintain recovery procedures

Where required:

* Individuals managing credentials may be subject to KYC

---

## 12. Continuity Statement

The DAO affirms that:

* It receives assets to continue and support the objectives of the Radix ecosystem
* It will manage those assets in alignment with its purpose

This statement must be publicly recorded.

---

## 13. Compliance

The legal entity must:

* Maintain good standing
* Comply with applicable regulations
* Support lawful operation of the DAO

---

## 14. Relationship to Governance Documents

This document:

* Complements the DAO Charter
* Does not override governance authority
* Enables real-world execution of DAO decisions

---

## 15. Amendments

This document may be updated via governance proposals.

---

## 16. Guiding Principle

> The legal structure exists to **enable the DAO**,
> not to control it.
