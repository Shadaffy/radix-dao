The Working Group Framework activation and uncontested WGs proceed on the current timeline. Each WG is structurally independent once established.

## 2. Summary

This proposal activates the Working Group Framework and establishes all five Phase 1 Working Groups — Governance & Legal, Protocol & Engineering, Treasury & Finance, Community & Marketing, and Infrastructure & Security — by formally adopting their charters. Steward appointments are excluded and follow immediately via a lightweight election process. Once operational, each Working Group's activation automatically ends the RAC's interim execution authority for that workstream under Transition Governance Framework §5.4. This is a prerequisite for Phase 2 entry: the Transition Governance Framework §10 requires all five Phase 1 Working Groups to have operated independently for at least 60 consecutive days.

## 4. Rationale

Working Groups are the DAO's primary execution bodies. Without them:

* The RAC must act as interim execution coordinator for all operational workstreams — this is a transitional arrangement that was never intended to be permanent
* No body has a formal mandate to maintain governance documentation, develop the protocol roadmap, manage treasury reporting, coordinate community engagement, or oversee security
* The Phase 1 completion criteria (TGF §10) require all five Working Groups to have operated independently for 60 consecutive days — the clock does not start until they are established
* RAC interim execution authority under TGF §5.4 remains active across all workstreams, creating a concentration of coordination power inconsistent with the DAO's decentralization principle

The Working Group Framework is the governance document that defines how Working Groups are created, governed, and dissolved. It must be activated (TGF §3.2 trigger) before any individual Working Group charter has formal standing. By including this activation in the same proposal as the WG charters, both are satisfied simultaneously with a single vote.

Bundling all five Phase 1 Working Groups saves approximately 84 days versus sequential proposals (four proposal cycles). The structures are not contentious — they are execution bodies with defined scopes, not governance or decision-making authorities. The tradeoff of bundle risk is mitigated by the Steward exclusion (removing the most debatable element) and the charter separation option described above.

Without this proposal:
* RAC interim authority continues indefinitely across all operational workstreams
* The DAO cannot move toward Phase 2 (60-day WG operation clock never starts)
* No formal execution body exists for treasury management, protocol development, community engagement, security coordination, or governance maintenance

Radix DLT DAO LLC is registered. The Working Group Framework has not been activated (TGF §3.2). No Working Groups exist. The RAC holds interim execution authority across all operational workstreams under TGF §5.4. Draft charters for all five Phase 1 Working Groups have been prepared and are published with this Temperature Check.

### 5.2 Proposed Change

This proposal enacts two types of action:

---

#### Action 1 — Activate Working Group Framework

The Working Group Framework (`Governance-Processes/Execution/Working-Group-Framework.md`) is hereby formally activated per Transition Governance Framework §3.2. From the date this vote passes:

* The Working Group Framework is a binding governance document governing all Working Group operations
* The Charter Template (`Governance-Processes/Execution/Working-Group-Charter-Template.md`) is the required format for all future WG charter amendments and new WG proposals
* Any future Working Group established by governance vote must comply with the Working Group Framework

#### Steward Appointment Process (Post-Vote)

Stewards are not appointed by this proposal. Immediately upon vote passage:

1. The RAC publishes a call for nominations for all five Working Groups simultaneously
2. A 5–7 day nomination period opens (per DAO Parameters §6B)
3. Stage 1 Approval Voting proposals open for each WG (community approves a shortlist of candidates)
4. Stage 2 confirmation votes confirm Steward appointments per the Election & Role Governance Policy
5. Each WG is operational once its Stewards are confirmed

To minimize delay, the community is encouraged to begin informal discussions of potential Steward candidates during the TC period (Days 37–43). Nominations are not binding during TC — this is community coordination, not a formal process.

**RAC interim authority timeline:** The RAC's interim execution authority (TGF §5.4) lapses for each workstream as its Working Group becomes operational. There is no formal handoff required — the authority lapses automatically. The RAC records each lapse in its quarterly delegation status report (TGF §9).

## 7. Risks and Trade-offs

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thin Steward candidate pool for one or more WGs | Medium | Medium | Begin informal community discussions during TC period; RAC publishes clear role description with TC; no WG is blocked from formation if Stewards take time to confirm |
| Specific WG charter scope contested | Low | Low | Charter separation option — contested WG proceeds as standalone follow-on; uncontested WGs and WG Framework proceed on schedule |
| Bundle fails due to single contested element | Low | Medium | RAC monitors TC sentiment and offers separation proactively before TC closes |
| Treasury & Finance and Security & Infrastructure WGs (new charters) receive pushback | Medium | Low | Both charters published with TC for full community review period; RAC responds to concerns; charter amendments can be proposed after establishment |
| WG operationalization significantly delayed | Low | Medium | RAC retains interim authority per workstream during delay; Phase 1 completion criteria clock starts when WG is operational, not when charter passes |
| Community & Ecosystem scope extension disputed | Low | Low | Scope extension is noted in the proposal text; Governance & Legal WG commits to formal charter amendment within 90 days |
| RAC interim authority handoff creates operational gaps | Low | Low | Handoff is automatic per TGF §5.4; no gaps — each WG assumes authority upon operationalization |

**Known dependencies:**

* **GP-PRE-1 must pass first:** GP-3 requires entity registration. If GP-PRE-1 fails or registration is delayed, GP-3 cannot proceed.
* **GP-3 is independent of GP-1 and GP-2:** Working Group establishment does not depend on BOIR filing, policy adoption, or the asset transfer. WGs can be established and operational regardless of GP-1 or GP-2 outcome.
* **Working Group Framework activation:** The Working Group Framework (TGF §3.2) must be activated before WG charters have formal standing. This proposal activates both simultaneously — the WG Framework and the five charters — so no prior activation is required.

* **Election & Role Governance Policy:** This policy activates "before the first role term expires and elections are needed" (TGF §3.2). Steward elections are the first significant elections under the DAO's system. The RAC must activate the Election & Role Governance Policy before opening Stage 1 voting for Steward elections if it has not been activated already.
