# Elections & Role Governance Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how key DAO roles are:

* created
* elected or appointed
* evaluated
* replaced or removed

It ensures that all operational and oversight roles remain:

* accountable to the DAO
* time-bound
* replaceable

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* Accountability Council (RAC) members
* Working Group Stewards
* Strategic Coordination WG members
* Authorized Signers

---

## 3. Guiding Principles

### 3.1 DAO Sovereignty

All roles derive authority from DAO governance.

---

### 3.2 Term Limits

All roles are time-bound and must be renewed.

---

### 3.3 Accountability

All role holders are accountable to the DAO.

---

### 3.4 Replaceability

Any role holder can be removed through governance.

---

### 3.5 Transparency

Selection, removal, and performance must be visible.

---

## 4. Role Categories

| Role Type              | Function                         |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| RAC Members            | Oversight & governance integrity |
| Working Group Stewards | Execution coordination           |
| Strategic WG           | Cross-DAO coordination           |
| Authorized Signers     | Treasury & legal execution       |

---

## 5. Eligibility Requirements

All candidates must:

* accept the DAO’s principles
* disclose conflicts of interest
* meet role-specific requirements
* be able to fulfill responsibilities

Additional requirements (e.g., KYC) may apply per role.

---

## 6. Nomination Process

### 6.1 Open Nominations

* Any eligible individual may self-nominate or be nominated
* Nominations must be public

---

### 6.2 Candidate Submission

Each candidate must provide:

* background and qualifications
* role-specific experience
* conflict disclosures
* intended contribution

---

## 7. Election Process

Elections follow the two-stage process defined in the Proposal & Voting Framework (§4.6).

---

### 7.1 Stage 1 — Shortlisting

A Signaling proposal using Approval Voting is published containing all eligible nominees.

* Each voter may select up to the number of open seats (see DAO Parameters §6B)
* The shortlist size equals the number of open seats multiplied by the shortlist multiplier (see DAO Parameters §6B)
* Candidates are ranked by total voting power received
* The top candidates by that ranking advance to Stage 2
* The Stage 1 result is procedurally binding: no substitution of shortlisted candidates is permitted outside the fallback process

---

### 7.2 Stage 2 — Confirmation

Each shortlisted candidate is put to a separate Standard (YES/NO/ABSTAIN) proposal.

* Stage 2 proposals may run concurrently
* A candidate is confirmed if they meet the approval threshold and quorum for Election proposals (DAO Parameters §3.2–3.3)

---

### 7.3 Fallback

If a shortlisted candidate fails their Stage 2 confirmation vote:

* The next-highest candidate from Stage 1 by total voting power is automatically advanced to a replacement confirmation vote
* This continues until all seats are filled or the shortlist is exhausted
* If the shortlist is exhausted without filling all seats, remaining vacancies trigger the vacancy handling process (§11)

---

### 7.4 Term Start

Confirmed role holders begin their term on the defined start date following the completion of all Stage 2 confirmation votes.

---

## 8. Term Lengths

| Role                   | Term     |
| ---------------------- | -------- |
| RAC Members            | 6 months |
| Working Group Stewards | 6 months |
| Strategic WG           | 6 months |
| Authorized Signers     | 6 months |

Terms may be adjusted via DAO Parameters.

---

## 9. Re-election

* Role holders may stand for re-election
* No automatic renewal
* Performance may be considered by voters

---

## 10. Removal & No-Confidence

### 10.1 Removal by Vote

Any role holder may be removed via governance vote.

---

### 10.2 Grounds for Removal

* misconduct
* inactivity
* breach of duty
* loss of trust

---

### 10.3 Immediate Suspension

Temporary suspension may occur via emergency procedures if:

* security risk exists
* misconduct is urgent
* operational continuity is threatened

---

## 11. Vacancy Handling

If a role becomes vacant:

* interim coverage may be assigned where necessary
* replacement election must be initiated

---

### 11.1 Time to Replacement

Defined in DAO Parameters.

---

## 12. Handover Requirements

Outgoing role holders must:

* provide status overview
* transfer documentation
* ensure continuity

---

## 13. Performance Expectations

Role holders must:

* fulfill responsibilities defined in their charters
* maintain communication and reporting
* remain reasonably available

---

## 14. Inactivity Rules

If a role holder becomes inactive:

* they may be flagged
* review may be triggered
* removal may follow

Thresholds defined in DAO Parameters.

---

## 15. Conflicts of Interest

Role holders must:

* disclose conflicts
* recuse where necessary
* avoid self-dealing

---

## 16. Multi-Role Participation

Individuals may hold multiple roles only if:

* conflicts are manageable
* no concentration of power occurs

Restrictions may apply via DAO Parameters.

---

## 17. Transition Phase Provisions

During initial DAO setup:

* roles may be temporarily appointed
* expedited processes may apply

All temporary roles must transition to elections within a defined period.

---

## 18. Governance Parameters

Defined separately:

* term lengths
* election durations
* removal thresholds
* inactivity thresholds
* vacancy timelines

---

## 19. Amendments

This policy may be updated via governance.

---

## 20. Guiding Principle

> No role is permanent.
> All authority flows from the DAO and returns to it.
