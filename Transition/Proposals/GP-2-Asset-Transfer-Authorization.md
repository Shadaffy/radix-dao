## 2. Summary

This proposal authorizes Radix DLT DAO LLC to formally receive the assets of the Radix ecosystem from the Founding Transferor by approving the Asset Transfer Agreement and its IP Schedule, and by authorizing the Legal Signatory to execute both documents on behalf of the DAO. It simultaneously activates the Open Source & IP Policy and Source Code Stewardship Policy — prerequisites under the Transition Governance Framework for receiving source code assets. The IP Schedule, enumerating every asset being transferred, is published with this Temperature Check for the mandatory minimum 14-day community review period.

## 4. Rationale

The Founding Transferor holds the intellectual property, source code, trademarks, and related assets that constitute the Radix protocol ecosystem. The DAO was created to steward and govern these assets for the benefit of the community. Until the transfer executes, the DAO holds no IP, controls no source code, and cannot exercise meaningful governance over the protocol it was formed to govern.

The Asset Transfer Agreement is the legal instrument that effects this transfer. It has been negotiated and agreed in principle during Phase 0 (Track D). The IP Schedule (Exhibit A) enumerates every specific asset — repositories with commit hashes, trademark registrations, patent applications, documentation, and third-party license rights.

Constitutional-level threshold (≥66%/≥10%) is appropriate because:

1. The transfer is irreversible — assets received cannot be easily returned if the community later objects
2. The IP Schedule commits the DAO to specific assets with specific encumbrances — the community must affirmatively accept any limitations
3. Simultaneously activating two governance policies constitutes a structural change requiring constitutional-level authorization
4. The asset transfer is the foundational act that gives the DAO meaningful substance beyond its legal shell

Without this proposal:
* The DAO holds no IP; it cannot govern the protocol, fund development, or exercise meaningful stewardship
* The Open Source & IP Policy and Source Code Stewardship Policy remain inactive; the RAC cannot issue any RFP with code deliverables
* The Continuity Statement cannot be executed; the Registered Agent does not have an asset continuity record

## 5. Proposal Description

### 5.1 Current State

Radix DLT DAO LLC is registered and has adopted its Phase 1 governance framework (GP-PRE-1). The DAO holds no IP assets. The Asset Transfer Agreement and IP Schedule have been negotiated with the Founding Transferor and are ready for execution. The Open Source & IP Policy and Source Code Stewardship Policy exist as governance documents but have not been activated.

### 5.2 Proposed Change

This proposal enacts four inseparable components:

---

#### Component 1 — Approve the Asset Transfer Agreement

The community approves the full text of the Asset Transfer Agreement (ATA) between:

* **Transferor:** [Founding Transferor legal name — to be published with TC]
* **Transferee:** Radix DLT DAO LLC (represented by Legal Signatories, authorized by this proposal)

**Key ATA provisions being approved:**

| Provision | Summary |
|---|---|
| Article 2 — Transfer of Assets | Transferor transfers all right, title, and interest in IP Schedule assets free and clear of encumbrances (except as disclosed) |
| Article 3 — IP Schedule | Exhibit A enumerates all specific assets; incomplete schedule is a material defect permitting rejection |
| Article 4 — Representations and Warranties | Transferor warrants: title and authority; no encumbrances (except disclosed); no infringement; license validity; source code completeness; compliance |
| Article 5 — Indemnification | Transferor indemnifies the DAO against third-party claims arising pre-transfer; 3-year survival (unlimited for fraud/willful misrepresentation) |
| Article 6 — Conditions Precedent | IP Schedule completed; governance authorization; custody systems operational; all Delegates KYC-verified; Continuity Statement prepared |
| Article 7 — Acceptance Procedure | Legal Signatories verify transfer; execute Acceptance Statement; publish within 72 hours |
| Article 8 — Disputes | Governed by Marshall Islands law; mediation then ICDR arbitration |

The full ATA text is published with this Temperature Check. Token holders are approving the specific text as written — the Legal Signatory has no authority to modify material provisions after this vote passes.

---

#### Component 2 — Approve the IP Schedule (Exhibit A)

The community approves the IP Schedule as the complete and accurate enumeration of all assets being transferred. The IP Schedule must include:

| Asset Category | Required Detail |
|---|---|
| Software repositories | Repository URL; commit hash or release tag at transfer date; applicable open-source or proprietary license |
| Third-party License Rights | Licensor name; applicable restrictions on assignment or sublicensing |
| Trademark registrations | Registration jurisdiction; registration number; current status |
| Patent applications / grants | Patent/application jurisdiction; number; current status |
| Documentation | Title; type (whitepaper/technical specification/other); version or date |
| Other IP | Description; type; license or ownership status |
| Encumbrances | Description of any liens, claims, or restrictions by asset |

**The IP Schedule is published alongside this TC.** Token holders have the full TC period and early vote period to review — a minimum of 14 days before the vote opens, as required by the Optimised Activation Roadmap. If the TC period alone does not satisfy the 14-day minimum, the vote opening is delayed until the full review period has elapsed.

If the IP Schedule is found to have a material defect (missing assets, incorrect commit hashes, undisclosed encumbrances), token holders should vote NO and the RAC will work with the Founding Transferor to produce a corrected schedule before resubmission.

---

#### Component 3 — Authorize Legal Signatory Execution

Authorize the Legal Signatory to execute the Asset Transfer Agreement and IP Schedule on behalf of Radix DLT DAO LLC upon vote passage, subject to the Pre-Execution Hold (48 hours after vote close per DAO Parameters §9, allowing the veto window to close).

The Legal Signatory is further authorized to:

* Execute the Continuity Statement on behalf of the DAO per Operating Agreement §3.2
* File the Continuity Statement with the Registered Agent (MIDAO Directory Services)
* Publish the Acceptance Statement to the governance forum within 72 hours of ATA execution
* Create the provenance record per Operating Agreement §9.3
* File an updated BOIR within 30 days if the asset transfer creates a change in reportable information

The Legal Signatory may not modify any material provision of the ATA after this vote. Any change to asset categories, representations, warranties, or indemnification terms requires a new governance proposal.

---

#### Component 4 — Activate Open Source & IP Policy and Source Code Stewardship Policy

Simultaneously with ATA execution, formally activate the following two policies (Transition Governance Framework §3.2 activity triggers):

| Policy | Path | Activation Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Open Source & IP Policy | `Governance-Processes/Core/OpenSource-and-Intellectual-Property-Policy.md` | Before first grant or RFP with deliverables; required before code assets can be received |
| Source Code Stewardship Policy | `Governance-Processes/Core/Smart-Contract-Audit-Policy.md` | Activates alongside Open Source & IP Policy |

**Effect of activation:** These policies become binding governance framework documents from the date this vote passes. The RAC may not issue any RFP with code deliverables, and the Legal Signatory may not execute the ATA for source code repositories, until these policies are active. By activating them in the same proposal, the dependency is satisfied simultaneously.

## 10. Conflict of Interest Disclosure

- [x] I have the following conflict(s) to declare:

**Founding Transferor relationship:** The Founding Transferor has had significant organizational and financial relationships with current Delegates and the RAC. These relationships have been disclosed through the governance process. The ATA includes indemnification provisions and arms-length terms to ensure the transfer is fair to the DAO.

**Legal Signatory self-authorization:** The Legal Signatory is authorized by this proposal to execute documents on behalf of the DAO — they are in effect being authorized to execute an agreement on their own behalf as a Delegate. This is mitigated by: (a) the specific terms of the ATA being approved by token holder vote, not the Legal Signatory; and (b) the Legal Signatory's authority being strictly bounded by the terms approved by this vote.

Any covered person (RAC member, Authorized Signer) who has a personal financial relationship with the Founding Transferor beyond their normal Delegate role must declare this prior to the vote and recuse from any execution-related decisions.
