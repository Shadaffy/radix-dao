# Protocol & Engineering Working Group Charter

---

## 1. Purpose

This Working Group exists to own the full software development lifecycle for the Radix protocol and core DAO software. It is responsible for:

* Protocol strategy, maintenance, and enhancement
* Engineering standards and repository governance
* Secure development practices across all DAO-controlled codebases
* Smart contract audit coordination prior to any governance deployment

It does not operate infrastructure or manage information security — those responsibilities sit with the Infrastructure & Security WG.

---

## 2. Scope

### In Scope

* Protocol and product roadmap — maintained and proposed to the DAO
* Coordination of development contributors and technical teams
* Delivery of network upgrades (e.g., Xi'an and beyond)
* Identification and prioritisation of technical improvements
* Source repository governance — branch protection, contribution standards, access controls for DAO-controlled repositories
* Secure development lifecycle standards — code review requirements, dependency management, release integrity
* Smart contract audit coordination — scoping, scheduling, and funding audits prior to any governance contract deployment
* RFPs for development and protocol work

### Out of Scope

* Cannot unilaterally change protocol rules — all protocol changes require DAO approval
* Cannot override DAO-approved priorities
* Cannot control treasury outside approved budgets
* Does not operate infrastructure or manage network/node operators — owned by Infrastructure & Security WG
* Does not run the vulnerability disclosure programme or bug bounty — owned by Infrastructure & Security WG

---

## 3. Responsibilities

* Develop and maintain a public protocol and product roadmap
* Coordinate contributors and technical teams against roadmap milestones
* Define and issue RFPs for development and protocol work
* Track milestone delivery and escalate blockers
* Govern DAO-controlled source repositories: access controls, branching standards, contribution guidelines
* Define and enforce secure development lifecycle standards across DAO-funded codebases
* Coordinate smart contract audits: scope definition, auditor selection (via RFP), scheduling, and publication of audit reports
* Engage the Infrastructure & Security WG on audit policy compliance and security standards input
* Identify technical risks and dependencies; escalate to RAC where necessary

---

## 4. Deliverables

* Public protocol and product roadmap (maintained continuously)
* Quarterly delivery plan with milestone tracking
* Technical RFPs
* Progress reports and development dashboards
* Source repository governance policy (published at WG activation)
* Secure development lifecycle standards document
* Smart contract audit coordination records — auditor, scope, findings, remediation status (public)

---

## 5. Leadership Structure

* 2–3 elected Stewards
* Term: 6 months
* Elected via DAO governance

---

## 6. Steward Responsibilities

* Maintain roadmap clarity and prioritisation
* Coordinate contributors and technical teams
* Ensure progress against milestones
* Own repository governance and secure development standards
* Coordinate audit engagements within approved budget
* Report to the DAO and escalate risks and blockers to the RAC

---

## 7. Decision-Making

* Internal: simple majority
* Quorum: 2 of 3

DAO approval required for:

* Major roadmap changes
* Budget increases
* Changes to approved audit scope or auditor selection above material threshold

---

## 8. Budget Authority

* Fund development work within approved budget
* Issue RFPs and allocate resources
* Fund smart contract audit engagements within approved budget
* No independent spending authority outside approved mandates

---

## 9. Reporting

* Monthly development updates — milestone status, blockers, budget status
* Quarterly retrospective
* Public dashboard — roadmap and delivery status
* Audit coordination records — updated on each engagement

---

## 10. Coordination

* Infrastructure & Security WG (audit policy compliance, security standards input, incident notification)
* Ecosystem Growth WG (builder and dApp technical alignment)
* Governance & Legal WG (proposal alignment, policy activation sequencing)
* Treasury & Finance WG (budget administration, RFP payment execution)
* RAC (compliance, risk escalation)

---

## 11. Conflicts of Interest

Stewards must:

* Disclose any personal, financial, or organisational interest in development contracts, auditors, or contributors funded by this WG
* Recuse themselves from decisions where a conflict exists
* Avoid self-dealing in auditor selection or RFP awards

All disclosures must be public and logged.

---

## 12. Renewal / Sunset

* Standard term: 6 months
* Renewal requires a new DAO governance proposal with updated charter, budget, and performance summary
* If not renewed, all active development contracts and open audit engagements must be documented and handed to the RAC or successor WG within 21 days; unspent funds return to treasury
* DAO may dissolve this WG at any time by governance vote; all spending authority ceases immediately

---

## 13. Guiding Principle

> This Working Group exists to **build, maintain, and continuously improve** the Radix protocol and core DAO software —
> with engineering rigour, open governance, and a secure development culture at its foundation.
