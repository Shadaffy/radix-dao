# Governance & Legal Support Working Group Charter

---

## 1. Purpose

This Working Group exists to support DAO governance operations, legal coordination, and documentation.

---

## 2. Scope

### In Scope

* Governance documentation and processes
* Legal coordination and compliance support
* Proposal support and governance tooling
* Transition support

### Out of Scope

* Cannot override governance decisions
* Cannot act as legal authority unless explicitly authorized

---

## 3. Responsibilities

* Maintain governance documentation
* Support proposal drafting and clarity
* Coordinate legal processes and advisors
* Support governance tooling and infrastructure
* Regulatory response coordination: act as the primary point of contact when a Delegate invokes the regulatory demand disclosure protocol (Legal Wrapper §14A); initiate DAO-authorized legal counsel engagement; maintain a standing budget line (or initiate an emergency Treasury & Budget proposal) to cover reasonable legal counsel costs for Delegates facing regulatory demands arising from their DAO duties

---

## 4. Deliverables

* Governance documentation updates
* Legal coordination reports
* Proposal support outputs
* Process improvements

---

## 5. Leadership Structure

* 2–3 elected Stewards
* Term: 6 months

---

## 6. Steward Responsibilities

* Maintain governance clarity
* Coordinate legal efforts
* Support community governance participation
* Ensure documentation accuracy

---

## 7. Decision-Making

* Internal: majority
* DAO approval for:

  * major governance changes
  * legal commitments

---

## 8. Budget Authority

* Legal services
* Governance tooling
* Documentation support

---

## 9. Reporting

* Monthly updates
* Governance status reports

---

## 10. Coordination

* RAC (critical interface for governance compliance and document review)
* All Working Groups (governance support and documentation)
* DAO community (proposal support and transparency)

---

## 11. Conflicts of Interest

Stewards must:

* Disclose any personal, financial, or organizational interest in governance decisions they support
* Recuse themselves from drafting or reviewing documents where a conflict exists
* Avoid using this WG's access to governance processes to benefit personal proposals

All disclosures must be public and logged.

---

## 12. Renewal / Sunset

* Standard term: 6 months
* Renewal requires a new DAO governance proposal with updated charter, budget, and performance summary
* If not renewed, all active responsibilities must be handed over to RAC or successor WG within 21 days
* DAO may dissolve this WG at any time by governance vote

## 13. Emergency Backstop Authority (Tier 2)

Notwithstanding §2 ("cannot act as legal authority unless explicitly authorized"), the Governance & Legal WG Stewards are explicitly authorized to act as Tier 2 fallback authority under the Governance Continuity Framework §4.1 when no RAC members are available to invoke Tier 1.

Under Tier 2, the WG Stewards (by internal majority vote) may:

* Declare a RAC continuity event on the governance forum
* Appoint interim signers from the Emergency Signer Reserve
* Initiate the emergency RAC election process

Restrictions:

* This authority activates only when zero RAC members are available — it does not activate simply because the RAC lacks quorum while members remain reachable
* WG Stewards may not appoint themselves or each other as interim signers or RAC members
* All Tier 2 actions require immediate public disclosure and a 24-hour challenge window before taking effect
* This authority does not extend to any other legal, treasury, or governance decision-making functions
* All Tier 2 actions are subject to GP ratification within 30 days; actions not ratified within 30 days are void
