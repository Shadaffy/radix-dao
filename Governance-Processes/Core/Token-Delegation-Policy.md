# Token Delegation Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how XRD token holders may delegate their voting power to other participants, and the obligations that apply to those who accept delegation.

Delegation is a core mechanism for sustaining active, informed governance participation in a large token-holder community. This policy:

* Establishes how delegation works and who may participate
* Defines the rights and responsibilities of delegates
* Sets transparency and accountability standards for delegates
* Defines how delegation interacts with other governance processes

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* **Delegators** — XRD token holders who assign their voting power to another participant
* **Delegates** — participants who accept and exercise voting power on behalf of delegators

This policy applies to governance votes conducted through the DAO's designated governance interface.

---

## 3. Delegation Mechanics

### 3.1 How Delegation Works

* A token holder may delegate their full voting power to any eligible delegate at any time
* Delegation is recorded on-chain and takes effect at the next governance snapshot after the delegation transaction
* A token holder may only delegate to a single delegate at a time
* A delegate may receive delegation from multiple token holders

---

### 3.2 Splitting and Partial Delegation

Partial delegation (splitting voting power across multiple delegates) is determined by the capabilities of the governance platform in use. Where the platform supports partial delegation, token holders may split their voting power across multiple delegates.

The DAO Parameters Registry records the current delegation mechanics supported by the active governance platform.

---

### 3.3 Snapshot Timing

Voting power — including delegated power — is calculated at the snapshot taken at the start of each voting period, per the **Proposal & Voting Framework §6.1**.

Delegation changes made after a snapshot has been taken do not affect the current vote.

---

### 3.4 Revocation

A delegator may revoke or change their delegation at any time. Revocation takes effect at the next governance snapshot.

---

### 3.5 Self-Delegation

Token holders who have previously delegated may reclaim direct voting rights by delegating to themselves.

---

## 4. Delegate Eligibility

Any participant may accept delegation, with the following exceptions:

* A participant who is currently suspended from DAO governance participation under the **Code of Conduct** enforcement process may not accept new delegation during the suspension period
* No eligibility restrictions apply to role-holders — RAC members, Working Group Stewards, and Authorized Signers may hold and exercise delegated voting power, subject to the conflict of interest obligations in Section 7

---

## 5. Delegate Responsibilities

Delegation is a relationship of trust. Delegates who publicly represent themselves as accepting delegation take on the following responsibilities:

### 5.1 Voting Participation

* Delegates are expected to vote on proposals during their active period
* Persistent non-participation (abstaining or failing to vote on a substantial portion of proposals) without communication undermines the purpose of delegation and should be disclosed to delegators

---

### 5.2 Communication

Delegates who accept public delegation are expected to:

* Make their governance positions and rationale accessible to delegators — through forum posts, delegate platforms, or other public channels
* Notify delegators of extended periods where they will be unavailable to vote

There is no mandatory format or frequency for delegate communications, but transparency is the expected standard.

---

### 5.3 Acting in Delegators' Interests

Delegates must exercise delegated voting power in good faith. Delegates must not:

* Vote in exchange for personal compensation or other benefits outside the normal governance process (vote-buying per the **Code of Conduct §4**)
* Coordinate voting in ways that harm the DAO or circumvent its governance processes
* Exercise delegated power to benefit themselves or affiliated entities without disclosure

---

## 6. Delegate Transparency

### 6.1 Public Delegate Registry

The DAO maintains a voluntary Public Delegate Registry where delegates may register a profile including:

* Name or pseudonym
* Governance philosophy and priorities
* Conflict of interest disclosures
* Contact or communication channel

Registration is voluntary. Unregistered delegates may still accept delegation, but registered delegates signal a higher accountability commitment.

The Governance & Legal Working Group is responsible for maintaining the Public Delegate Registry.

---

### 6.2 On-Chain Visibility

All delegation relationships and voting actions are visible on-chain. The DAO's governance interface should display delegated voting power alongside direct voting power for transparency.

---

## 7. Conflicts of Interest

### 7.1 Role-Holders as Delegates

Role-holders (RAC members, Stewards, Authorized Signers) may hold delegated voting power. However:

* They must disclose this in their standing **Conflict of Interest Policy** disclosure
* Where their delegated voting power would be used on a matter where they have a personal conflict, they must recuse from that vote entirely — including the delegated portion
* They may not use delegated voting power to pass decisions that benefit themselves in ways that would constitute self-dealing

---

### 7.2 Large Delegation Concentration

The DAO monitors delegation concentration. Where a single delegate accumulates voting power that approaches or exceeds the Constitutional proposal quorum (currently 10% of circulating XRD), the RAC may:

* Flag the concentration to the community
* Recommend that delegators review their delegation

This is an advisory mechanism, not a hard cap. Hard caps on delegation, if required, must be approved by governance.

---

## 8. Delegator Rights

Delegators retain the following rights at all times:

* **Right to revoke** — delegation may be revoked at any point with immediate effect from the next snapshot
* **Right to vote directly** — if the governance platform permits, a delegator may override their delegate and vote directly on a specific proposal
* **Right to information** — delegators are entitled to understand how their delegate has voted via public on-chain records
* **Right to accountability** — delegators may raise concerns about delegate conduct via the reporting mechanisms in the **Code of Conduct §5**

---

## 9. Relationship to Voting Mechanics

* Delegated votes count identically to direct votes for all quorum and approval threshold calculations
* A delegate who is subject to a **Code of Conduct** sanction restricting governance participation loses the ability to exercise delegated voting power during the sanction period
* Delegation does not affect a token holder's eligibility to submit proposals or file veto challenges — these rights are tied to direct holdings, not delegated power

---

## 10. Relationship to Other Policies

* **Proposal & Voting Framework §6** — Snapshot mechanics and voting power calculation
* **Code of Conduct §4** — Prohibitions on vote-buying and collusion applicable to delegates
* **Conflict of Interest Policy** — Disclosure obligations for delegates who are also role-holders
* **DAO Parameters Registry §8** — Records current delegation mechanics supported by the governance platform

---
