# Governance Overview

# Radix DAO Governance Structure

The Radix DAO operates using a **layered governance model**, designed to balance:

* decentralization of authority
* operational clarity
* real-world execution capability
* long-term adaptability

---

## 🧠 Governance Layers

The system is structured into four core layers:

### 🧠 Charter (Principles)

Defines the foundational rules of the DAO, including:

* purpose and objectives
* governance authority
* non-distribution principles
* treasury stewardship
* core safeguards

The Charter is the **highest authority** and changes only through rigorous governance.

---

### ⚙️ Governance Processes

Defines how the DAO operates in practice:

* how proposals are made and approved
* how funds are allocated and executed
* how roles are assigned and governed
* how disputes, emergencies, and failures are handled
* how governance evolves over time

These documents are modular and adaptable.

---

### ⚖️ Legal & Representation

Defines how the DAO operates in the real world:

* legal entity structure (DAO LLC)
* asset custody and control
* contractual representation
* transition from the Foundation

---

### 👥 Execution Layer

Defines how decisions are carried out:

* Working Groups coordinate execution
* contributors deliver work
* Authorized Signers execute treasury and legal actions

Execution is separated from decision-making authority.

---

## 🧭 Design Principles

This layered structure ensures:

* **Clear separation of authority**
* **No hidden power concentration**
* **Operational flexibility**
* **Resilience under failure conditions**
* **Ability to evolve without breaking the system**

Each layer is documented separately and interacts through defined interfaces.

## 🧪 Phase 1 (Initial 12-Month Operation)

During the first 12 months, the DAO operates under a **simplified activation** of this framework:

* core governance systems are active
* some processes are simplified
* limited coordination responsibilities are temporarily delegated

This allows the DAO to:

* operate effectively from day one
* reduce early complexity
* transition safely from the Foundation

All simplifications are temporary and defined in the **Transition Governance Framework**.

---

## 🧩 Modularity

Each document in this structure is:

* focused on a single responsibility
* independently updatable (within governance rules)
* connected through clearly defined references

This enables:

* easier contribution
* safer upgrades
* better long-term maintainability

---

## 💬 On Structure & Readability

The Charter is intentionally concise and principle-based.

Detailed rules are moved into governance process documents to:

* improve readability
* reduce complexity in the Charter
* allow faster iteration where appropriate

The community is encouraged to propose improvements to structure, clarity, and usability over time.

---

## 🧠 Guiding Principle

> The governance system is designed to be:
>
> * simple enough to understand
> * structured enough to operate
> * flexible enough to evolve
