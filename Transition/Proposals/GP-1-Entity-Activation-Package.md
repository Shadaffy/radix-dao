## 2. Summary

This proposal activates Radix DLT DAO LLC as a compliant, legally operative entity by authorizing two time-sensitive administrative actions: filing the initial Beneficial Owner Information Report (BOIR) with the Registered Agent to satisfy the Marshall Islands DAO Act §712(1) 30-day requirement, and formally adopting all 10 Phase 1 governance documents as binding subordinate operational policies of the LLC under Operating Agreement §12.4.

## 4. Rationale

Entity registration creates two immediate, time-sensitive obligations:

**BOIR filing:** The Marshall Islands DAO Act §712(1) requires filing a Beneficial Owner Information Report within 30 days of formation. This is a legal compliance obligation — failure to file exposes the entity to civil penalties (up to $500/day) and potential criminal liability. The standard TC+vote cycle (7+14 days) fits within this window. However, if any delay pushes the filing close to the 30-day deadline, the RAC will file under transitional authority and this vote serves as retrospective ratification.

**Policy adoption under OA §12.4:** The Operating Agreement grants token holders authority to adopt governance documents as binding subordinate operational policies of the LLC. GP-PRE-1 adopted these documents as the governance framework; this proposal formally elevates them to binding LLC policy — giving them legal force in the Operating Agreement hierarchy, not just in governance. This step is necessary before the framework can be invoked in any legal proceeding or regulatory context.

Without this proposal:
* The entity is non-compliant with Marshall Islands annual reporting requirements
* The governance framework has governance force but not full LLC legal force

## 5. Proposal Description

### 5.1 Current State

Radix DLT DAO LLC is registered. The BOIR has not been filed. The 10 Phase 1 governance documents were adopted as governance framework by GP-PRE-1 but have not been formally adopted as binding LLC operational policies under Operating Agreement §12.4.

### 5.2 Proposed Change

This proposal enacts two administrative actions:

---

#### Element 1 — BOIR Filing Authorization

Authorize the Compliance Liaison to file the initial Beneficial Owner Information Report (BOIR) with MIDAO Directory Services per Marshall Islands DAO Act §712(1).

**Reportable persons:** All founding Delegates are listed as reportable persons under DAO Act §702(r), which classifies individuals exercising actual control over a DAO LLC as beneficial owners for reporting purposes regardless of token holdings. Additionally, any token holder confirmed as holding ≥25% of eligible voting power at registration must be identified and reported per the Legal Wrapper & Representation Policy §9.7.

**BOIR contents per §712(1) requirements:**

| Field | Required Information |
|---|---|
| Full legal name | Per KYC records on file |
| Date of birth | Per KYC records on file |
| Current residential address | Per KYC records on file |
| Non-expired passport number | Per KYC records on file |
| Associated wallet address(es) | On-chain address(es) used for governance and treasury |
| Delegate function(s) held | As ratified by GP-PRE-1 Element 3 |

**Retrospective filing fallback:** The BOIR must be filed within 30 days of formation per DAO Act §712(1). If the TC+vote cycle would push the filing deadline, the Compliance Liaison files under RAC transitional authority immediately post-registration, and this vote serves as retrospective community ratification of that filing. The RAC publishes a notice to the governance forum within 72 hours of any pre-vote filing.

---

Formally adopt the following 10 documents as binding subordinate operational policies of Radix DLT DAO LLC pursuant to Operating Agreement §12.4. This elevates them from governance framework documents (adopted by GP-PRE-1) to legally binding LLC policies:

## 10. Conflict of Interest Disclosure

- [x] I have the following conflict(s) to declare:

**Delegate reporting interest:** All founding Delegates are listed in the BOIR as reportable persons. They have a personal interest in how their information is recorded and managed. This is unavoidable for a compliance filing. Mitigation: the BOIR is filed with the Registered Agent (MIDAO) per Marshall Islands law confidentiality standards; the existence and KYC completion status of each Delegate is publicly disclosed, but the specific personal details are held by the Registered Agent in compliance with applicable law.
