# Strategic Coordination Working Group Charter

---

## 1. Purpose

This Working Group exists to:

* Improve coordination across Working Groups
* Facilitate coherent strategy formation
* Synthesize inputs into clear strategic direction proposals

It does not own or control strategy, but ensures that:

> Strategy is visible, aligned, and actionable.

---

## 2. Scope

### In Scope

* Cross-working group coordination
* Strategic synthesis and roadmap alignment
* Identification of gaps, overlaps, and conflicts
* Facilitation of planning cycles
* Publishing ecosystem-wide strategic views

---

### Out of Scope

This Working Group:

* Cannot define strategy unilaterally
* Cannot override other Working Groups
* Cannot approve or reject proposals
* Cannot control budget allocation outside its own

---

## 3. Responsibilities

* Aggregate inputs from all Working Groups
* Maintain a unified strategic overview
* Highlight risks, misalignment, and dependencies
* Facilitate periodic planning cycles
* Support development of DAO-wide strategy proposals
* Monitor rolling treasury outflow and publish a mandatory Signaling proposal ("Treasury Outflow Alert") when trailing 90-day disbursements across all Treasury / Budget proposals exceed 15% of treasury value at the last quarterly snapshot. This alert is not a veto; it requires the community to formally acknowledge the spending rate (via a recorded acknowledgement on the governance forum) before the next Treasury / Budget proposal's voting period can open. The RAC pauses that voting period until acknowledgement is recorded (RAC Mandate §2).

---

## 4. Deliverables

* DAO Strategic Overview (living document)
* Quarterly Strategic Alignment Report
* Cross-WG roadmap synthesis
* Identified priorities and gaps
* Strategic proposal drafts (for DAO voting)
* Treasury Outflow Alert (triggered, not scheduled) — published when the 15% trailing 90-day threshold is met

---

## 5. Leadership Structure

* 2 elected Strategists / Coordinators
* Term: 6 months
* Elected via DAO governance

---

## 6. Steward Responsibilities

Strategists are responsible for:

* Coordinating across Working Groups
* Synthesizing strategy inputs
* Facilitating alignment discussions
* Publishing strategic insights

They are **not responsible for execution** of all work.

---

## 7. Decision-Making

* Internal decisions: consensus preferred, majority if needed
* No authority over other Working Groups

DAO approval required for:

* Any strategic proposal
* Any changes affecting DAO-wide priorities

---

## 8. Budget Authority

* Limited to coordination activities
* Research, analysis, facilitation
* No authority over other WG budgets

---

## 9. Reporting

* Monthly coordination updates
* Quarterly strategic reports
* Public strategic dashboard

---

## 10. Coordination Role

This Working Group acts as:

* A coordination layer between Working Groups
* A facilitator of DAO-wide planning

It must:

* Engage all Working Groups regularly
* Ensure transparency of discussions
* Avoid centralization of influence

---

## 11. Interaction with Other Working Groups

* Works collaboratively, not hierarchically
* Cannot impose decisions
* Supports alignment and visibility

---

## 12. Interaction with RAC

* RAC ensures this group does not exceed its mandate
* No special authority granted

---

## 13. Risks & Safeguards

### Risk: Centralization of Strategy

Mitigation:

* No decision authority
* DAO approval required

---

### Risk: Becoming a “Shadow Executive”

Mitigation:

* Clear scope limitations
* Transparency of all outputs

---

## 14. Renewal / Sunset

* 6-month term
* Requires DAO renewal

---

## 15. Guiding Principle

> This Working Group exists to **connect thinking**,
> not to **control direction**.
