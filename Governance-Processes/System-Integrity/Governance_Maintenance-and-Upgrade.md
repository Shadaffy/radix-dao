# Governance Maintenance & Upgrade Framework

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how governance documents are:

* maintained
* updated
* evolved over time

---

## 2. Guiding Principle

> Governance must be adaptable without compromising integrity.

---

## 3. Document Hierarchy

Governance documents follow this hierarchy:

1. Charter (highest authority)
2. Governance Policies
3. DAO Parameters Registry

If conflicts arise:

* higher-level documents prevail

---

## 4. Types of Changes

---

### 4.1 Patch Changes

* cosmetic or non-semantic
* do not affect meaning (e.g., typo corrections, formatting, cross-reference updates)

Approval:

* **Governance Process proposal** — standard threshold (≥60% YES, ≥7% quorum)

---

### 4.2 Minor Changes

* process improvements
* parameter adjustments
* non-structural updates to policy language

Approval:

* **Governance Process proposal** — standard threshold (≥60% YES, ≥7% quorum)

---

### 4.3 Major Changes

* changes to the Charter
* changes to governance structure
* changes affecting authority, rights, or the role of any governance body

Approval:

* **Constitutional proposal** — supermajority threshold (≥66% YES, ≥10% quorum)

All thresholds are defined in DAO Parameters §3.2 and §3.3.

---

## 5. Amendment Process

All changes must:

* be proposed through governance
* include clear rationale
* specify change type (patch / minor / major)
* include version updates

---

## 6. Versioning

Each update must include:

* version number
* summary of changes
* effective date

---

## 7. Consistency Rules

Changes must:

* not conflict with higher-level documents
* maintain internal consistency across policies
* clearly state any dependencies

---

## 8. Maintenance Responsibility

* Governance & Legal Support WG maintains documents
* RAC ensures process compliance
* DAO approves all substantive changes

---

## 9. Emergency Amendments

In urgent cases:

* Emergency Policy may apply
* temporary measures allowed
* must be ratified by DAO

---

## 10. Transparency

All changes must be:

* publicly visible
* clearly documented
* traceable over time

---

## 11. Guiding Principle

> Governance evolves through **clear rules**, not ad-hoc decisions.
